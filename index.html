I am an Assistant Professor in the Department of Mathematics at Dev Bhoomi Uttarakhand University. I received my Ph.D. in Applied Mathematics from PDPM IIITDM Jabalpur in 2025, and my M.Sc. in Mathematics from the Indian Institute of Technology Ropar. My research focuses on spectral and pseudospectral methods for time-dependent nonlinear partial differential equations, with applications in fluid dynamics, fractional calculus, and computational mathematics.

Research Interests

My research lies at the intersection of numerical analysis, applied mathematics, and the theory of partial differential equations. I develop high-order, computationally efficient algorithms for nonlinear and fractional PDEs, particularly problems where classical finite-difference and finite-element methods struggle with accuracy, stability, or convergence.

Spectral & Pseudospectral Methods

Legendre and Chebyshev pseudospectral discretization schemes for high-accuracy approximation of time-dependent PDEs.

Nonlinear Evolution Equations

Numerical analysis of multidimensional Burgers, Burgers–Fisher, and generalized nonlinear parabolic equations with steep gradients.

Fractional Differential Equations

Spectral approximations for fractional PDEs including the fractional cable and fractional Burgers equations based on the Caputo derivative.

Filter-Enhanced Collocation

Fast algorithms with spectral filtering for polynomial collocation methods that handle non-smooth and discontinuous solutions.

Computational Fluid Dynamics

High-order numerical schemes for quasi-linear fluid dynamics problems and convection-dominated flow regimes.

Mathematical Modelling

Oscillation dynamics in ecological metapopulation models, including Allee-effect studies and bifurcation analysis in coupled nonlinear systems.
